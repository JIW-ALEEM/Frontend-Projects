Guess Who

HOW TO PLAY GUESS WHO

Games using blindfolds have been popular team building activities for many years.[1] Blindfold games are a fun way to challenge your other senses without relying on sight. This is a great game to break down social barriers and create new friends. Try this game at a birthday party, sleepover, or as a classroom or summer camp activity. This is a blindfolded guessing game geared for pre-teens and teens.

Instructions

1 Spread out.

Tell everyone to find a spot in the room where they cannot touch anyone else. Have them sit down.


2 Conduct a quick icebreaker.

If you are playing with a group of people who don't know each other well, an icebreaker is a great way for everyone to meet.[3] If your group is shy, you can start by introducing yourself first. Ask each person to share: Their name. Their favorite hobby. One interesting fact about themselves. You may not need to conduct an icebreaker if everyone is already good friends.

3 Divide the group in half.

Instruct half of the people to put on their blindfolds. The blindfolded group will be the "guessers" first.

4 Begin the game by playing some party music.

Instruct the blindfolded group to stand up and begin walking slowly until they find a non-blindfolded partner. Each person who is not blindfolded should stand in the path of a blindfolded player. You may need to help everyone find a partner. Remind the blindfolded group to reach gently and slowly in the space in front of them and avoid swinging their arms around.

5 Give the blindfolded group a chance to feel their partner's features.

Remind everyone that this game is to be played in silence. Say, "Once you have found a partner, please explore the features of that partner with your hands. Remember always to be appropriate with the way you touch one another."[4]

6 Stop the music after about 30 seconds.

Instruct everyone that the guessing will begin. Ask each blindfolded player to tell you who his or her partner is. If everyone has just met, ask the blindfolded people to describe their partner if they don't remember their partner's name.

7 Instruct the blindfolded group to remove their blindfolds.

Watch their reactions as they discover who their partner is. Each person who succeeds in guessing their partner's identity, or is able to describe what they look like, wins.

8 Switch roles.

Have the other group put on their blindfolds and play the game again. Keep switching roles and play as many times as you like.
