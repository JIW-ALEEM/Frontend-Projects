SUZUKI

1.Suzuki GSX-R750:

The bike is powered by a 4-stroke, 4-cylinder 749cc engine. This DOHC engine produces a peak power of 147.8 bhp at 13200 rpm. This liquid-cooled engine generates a maximum torque output of 86.3 Nm which arrives at 11200 rpm.
Engine 749 cc, 4-stroke, liquid-cooled, DOHC, 4 valves per cylinder, inline-4
Bore / stroke: 70.0 mm × 48.7 mm (2.76 in × 1.92 in)
Power: 110.3 kW (147.9 hp; 150.0 PS) @ 12,800 rpm
Torque: 86.3 N⋅m (63.7 lbf⋅ft) @ 11,200 rpm
Transmission: 6-speed constant-mesh manual, chain-drive, wet, multi-plate clutch
Weight: 419 lbs / 190 kg (dry)





2.Suzuki GSX-R1000:

The Suzuki GSX-R1000 is a supersport motorcycle made by Suzuki. It was introduced in 2001 to replace the GSX-R1100 and is powered by a liquid-cooled 999 cc (61.0 cu in) inline four-cylinder, four-stroke engine although originally 988 cc (60.3 cu in) from 2001 to 2004.
Max Torque: 117.6 Nm @ 10800 rpm
Front Brake: Disc
Rear Brake: Disc
Engine Type: 4-Stroke 4-Cylinder, Liquid liquid-cooled
Body Type: Super Bikes
Max Power: 199.23 bhp @ 13200 rpm
Fuel Capacity: 16 Ltrs





3.Suzuki RGV250:

The top speed of a standard RGV250 is around 130 mph (209 km/h). It has a 0-60 mph (97 km/h) time of around 3.7 seconds.
Engine 249 cc (15.2 cu in) two-stroke 90° V-Twin
Bore / stroke: 54 mm × 54.5 mm (2.13 in × 2.15 in)
Power: 62 hp (46 kW)
Transmission: Six-speed manual
Brakes: Drilled vented discs front and rear
Wheelbase: 54.3 in (1,380 mm)
Weight: 305 lb (138 kg) (dry) 345 lb (156 kg) (wet)





4.Suzuki GSX1300R Hayabusa:

The Suzuki GSX1300R Hayabusa is a sports motorcycle made by Suzuki since 1999. It immediately won acclaim as the world's fastest production motorcycle, with a top speed of 303 to 312 km/h (188 to 194 mph).
Displacement: 1340 cc
Engine Type: 4-Stroke Liquid-cooled, DOHC,in-line four
No. of Cylinders: 4
Max Torque: 150 Nm @ 7000 rpm
Body Type: Super Bikes
Max Power: 190 PS @ 9700 rpm
No. of Cylinders: 4
